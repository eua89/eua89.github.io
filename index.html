Our Favourite Places In Greece

Here follow some or our favourite places in Greece that we have visited together, as well as some tips in case you decide to visit them. Do not hesitate to ask for more info or tips for Athens or other destinations.

Milos Island

  • Spend a day in Kimolos
  • Cruise with a sailboat to discover the southern part of the island
  • Eat watermelon pie
  • They prepare amazing dishes with local lam and sheep
  • Try the different types of local cheese
  • If you decide to go there ask us for more information about places to visit and eat

Sifnos Island

  • Sifnos has a huge pottery tradition
  • Medival town of Kastro
  • Try revithada (cheakpea stew), caprese salad and mastelo
  • They prepare amazing dishes with local lam and sheep
  • Try the different types of local cheese
  • If you decide to go there ask us for more information about places to visit and eat

Kythera Island

  • One of the birthplaces of Aphrodite
  • Apparent Venetian influences with the style found in Crete and Mani
  • Take the boat for a short tirip to Polyaigos
  • Hike the ravine of Mylopotamos and swim in the waterfall



Paxos Island

  • Paxos is the smallest island of the Ionian sea
  • Rent a boat to go to different beaches
  • Go to Anti-Paxos
  • It is said that Poseidon wishing to find a nice place to live with his lover Amphitrite struck his trtdent into the sea and Paxos rose from the depths and took shape


Monemvasia

  • Castle-town situated in the south-east of the Peloponnese
  • An earthquake separated Monemvasia from the mainland in 375 AD.
  • In its history, Monemvasia has been under Byzantine, Venetian and Ottoman rule
  • Today, Monemvasia only has around 15 permanent residents
  • Visit Mistra castles and Diros caves

Meteora

  • UNESCO World Heritage
  • Second most important historical site in Greece
  • Baskets and ropes were used to take people up and down the monasteries
  • Pillars were formatted about 60 million years ago
  • Meteora is derived from the ancient Greek word metéōros, meaning “raised from the ground, hanging, lofty”
